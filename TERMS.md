**Engineering Trustworthy Industrial AI**

Version: 1.0

Status: Living Document

Last Updated: July 2026

# Purpose

This document defines the authoritative terminology used throughout the Engineering Trustworthy Industrial AI research initiative.

Its objectives are to:

- establish a common vocabulary
- eliminate ambiguity
- provide canonical definitions
- maintain consistency across articles, books, talks, software and reference architectures
- support long-term citation and adoption

Definitions contained in this document supersede earlier informal usage unless explicitly noted.

---

# Research Status Definitions

| Status | Meaning |
|---------|---------|
| **Foundational** | Core principles that define the long-term vision of the research initiative. Expected to remain stable over many years. |
| **Canonical** | Official concepts, frameworks, or terminology formally introduced and recommended for use. |
| **Emerging** | Publicly introduced concepts that continue to evolve through research and feedback. |
| **Working** | Active research ideas undergoing refinement. Suitable for discussion but not yet considered stable. |
| **Experimental** | Early hypotheses or prototypes shared to stimulate discussion. |
| **Deprecated** | Historical terminology retained for context but no longer recommended. |

# Foundational Concepts

## Engineering Trustworthy Industrial AI

**Status:** Foundational
**Version:** 1.0



**Definition**

An engineering discipline focused on designing Industrial AI systems that earn trust through engineering knowledge, grounded reasoning, decision governance, human accountability and operational rigor.

---

## Engineering Knowledge

**Status:** Foundational
**Version:** 1.0


**Definition**

The body of engineering expertise—including standards, procedures, physical principles, operational practices, asset knowledge, failure mechanisms and institutional experience—that provides the foundation for trustworthy Industrial AI.

---

## Grounded Reasoning

**Status:** Foundational
**Version:** 1.0


**Definition**

Reasoning constrained by engineering knowledge, standards, physical principles and operational context rather than statistical correlation alone.

Grounded reasoning precedes trustworthy operational decisions.

---

# Canonical Frameworks

## The Governed Decision Layer

**Status:** Canonical
**Version:** 1.0


**First Introduced**

Volume I — The Governed Decision Layer

**Definition**

The architectural layer between reasoning and operational action responsible for validating, governing, explaining, authorizing and recording industrial decisions.

---

## ASSET-grade

**Status:** Canonical
**Version:** 1.0


**Definition**

A benchmark describing Industrial AI outputs that are:

- Auditable
- Specific
- Standards-aligned
- Explainable
- Trusted

The acronym is fixed.

Additional letters should not be appended.

---

## Trustworthy Decision

**Status:** Canonical
**Version:** 1.0


**Definition**

A decision that is sufficiently grounded, explainable, governed, auditable and operationally appropriate for industrial use.

Trustworthiness is an emergent engineering property rather than a model characteristic.

---

# Emerging Concepts

## Earned Autonomy

**Status:** Emerging


**Definition**

The principle that autonomous capability should be granted progressively through demonstrated evidence of trustworthy performance.

Autonomy is earned—not configured.

---

# Working Concepts

## Deterministic Decision Envelope

**Status:** Working

**Definition**

A deterministic operational boundary surrounding non-deterministic AI reasoning, ensuring predictable and accountable operational commitments.

This term refers to operational decisions—not deterministic AI.

---

## "Who Signs?"

**Status:** Canonical
**Version:** 1.0


**Definition**

A recurring design test asking whether responsibility for an AI-assisted operational decision has been clearly assigned.

If nobody signs, the decision is not operationally ready.
